5. Privacy

RPNow is inherently anonymous. The only personal information that we have access to is whatever you type into the chat, and the IP address through which you visit and use the site.

We will not disclose any of this information to any third parties. We may, however, occasionally monitor a few randomly selected roleplays. We want to undertsand how people are using this site, so that we can improve it to better suit your needs. We also must moderate the content on our site, to ensure the service is not used for any purposes which violate our terms of service. We may also generate statistical information which we may display publicly; however, no publicly available statistics will include specific content from individual roleplays unless we first get your consent.

We have taken multiple precautions to ensure that your data is secure, such as using SSL encryption and being very careful to prevent common web service attacks. However, as with any web service, there is always a small possibility of RPNow's security being compromised. If we become aware of any security breach, we will let you know within 48 hours. Because we do not collect e-mail addresses or any other methods of contact for our users, we will post an announcement on the front page of this site, as well as on the individual RP pages. We ask that our users avoid posting sensitive or personally identifying content in their RPs, to ensure that nothing of that nature is found, should an attacker breach the site's defenses.

Other than that, RPs are only as private as you keep them— anyone who has a link to your RP will be able to post to it, as well as view the entirety of its contents. We advise you to be cautious of how and with whom you share the URL for your RP. If, through no fault of our own, any unintended persons discover a link you posted to your RP, we will not be responsible for any damages caused. By sharing your roleplay's URL in any publicly viewable space, you waive our responsibility for any damages that any unintended participants may cause.
